## Multi Tenant

SpaceDF uses a multi-tenant architecture, allowing multiple customers to run on a single platform while keeping data, users, and configurations fully isolated.

Each tenant represents a separate organization or customer environment, operating independently to ensure security and privacy.

Each tenant has its own isolated environment, meaning:

- Data is not shared between tenants
- Users can only access their own resources
- Actions in one tenant do not affect others

This tenant-based structure enables secure, scalable, and well-organized management of multiple customers in SpaceDF.

## What is an Organization?

An **Organization** is the top-level container in SpaceDF where you can manage all your Spaces, devices, users, dashboards, data, and integrations in one centralized place.

An Organization can contain **multiple Spaces**, each representing an environment, project, or specific use case.

Key components within an Organization include:

- **Spaces:** Logical containers for devices, members, and dashboards.
- **Devices:** Hardware added to Spaces for tracking, monitoring, and integration.
- **Members:** Users with assigned roles and permissions within its Spaces.
- **Data & Dashboards:** Collected data from devices, visualized in dashboards for monitoring and analysis.
- **Configuration Settings:** Organization-wide and Space-specific settings that govern behavior and access.

By structuring resources this way, an Organization ensures that each team or customer's data and operations are isolated while still providing a centralized overview for management.

## Organization Management

**Organization Management** is the central area where you manage all **Organizations** (Workspaces) within the system. Organization Management is also where the platform's **multi-tenancy** capabilities are implemented and maintained.

You will automatically access Organization Management after logging into your account through [spacedf.com](https://spacedf.com/)

With the Organization Management site, you can:

- Create, delete, and manage all Organizations
- Configure the URL Slug Name - the unique link to each Organization's Space
- Set up organization-specific preferences and configurations

## Administration & Staff

At the **Organization level**, SpaceDF provides clear, role-based access to manage users, resources, and configurations securely.

### Admin (Organization Owner)

Admins have full control over the Organization, including:

- Managing organization settings and branding
- Adding, removing, and managing users
- Assigning roles and permissions
- Managing Spaces, devices, and integrations
- Accessing billing and system-level configurations

Admins are responsible for the overall structure, security, and operation of the Organization.

### Staff

Staff members support day-to-day operations within the Organization. Depending on their assigned role, they can:

- Manage Spaces and devices
- View and access data
- Configurable permissions

Staff access is limited to the permissions granted by Admins.

## Device Hub

The **Device Hub** is the central place where you manage all devices within your Organization.

From here, you can view device details, check their connection status, publish data, and organize inventory before assigning devices to users or locations.

The page is divided into two main tabs:

### Inventory

The Inventory is used to manage and organize devices before deployment. When a device is added, it is automatically stored in the Inventory. These are devices that have been added but have not yet been assigned to any Space.

Admins can also enable **Public Device** on this tab, allowing it to be shared device on the map with all users in the Organization.

### Devices

When devices in the Inventory are added by users to a Space, their status automatically changes to **Active** and they move to the Devices tab, ready for monitoring, data collection, and management within the Space.

## White Label

**White Label** in SpaceDF allows you to run the platform under **your own brand**. From the end user's perspective, they are using **your platform**, not SpaceDF.

With White Label, you can:

- Customize the platform name, logo, and brand identity
- Access SpaceDF via your own **domain or subdomain**
- Use branded **email templates**
- Apply **API branding** for external integrations
